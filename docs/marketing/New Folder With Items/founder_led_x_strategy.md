## 2. Content Pillars: The 5-Type Tweet Strategy

### Pillar 1: "Daily Build Updates" (40% of content)
**Format:** Short, transparent progress updates

**Examples:**
- "Day 23 of building LearnifyTube: Finally fixed the macOS permission bug that haunted me for 3 weeks. The issue? Electron's security model + SQLite file paths. Here's what I learned: [mini-thread]"
- "Rewrote the video player 4 times. Each version taught me something. Here's what each version got wrong (and why)..."
- "Just hit 1,000 lines of TypeScript deleted. Simplified my category system from 5 files to 1 function. Code: [gist link]"

**Goal:** Show consistent work, not consistent wins.

### Pillar 2: "Technical Lessons" (30% of content)
**Format:** Actionable dev tips from your journey

**Examples:**
- "üßµ Thread: 5 SQLite mistakes I made building a local-first app (and how to avoid them)"

**Goal:** Listen more than you speak. Build relationships.

### Pillar 5: "Celebration & Gratitude" (5% of content)
**Format:** Small wins and thank yous

**Examples:**
- "Someone opened their first issue on my repo today. This is why I love open source."

- "Milestone: LearnifyTube can now handle 10,000 videos without crashing. Not glamorous, but it matters."

**Goal:** Show you appreciate the community.

## 3. Posting Strategy: Consistency Over Virality

**Frequency:**
- **Minimum:** 2 tweets/day (1 build update + 1 lesson/engagement)
- **Optimal:** 3-4 tweets/day
- **Maximum:** Never more than 6/day (avoid spam perception)

**Timing:**
- **Morning (7-9 AM PT):** "What I'm working on today" type updates
- **Afternoon (12-2 PM PT):** Technical lessons/threads
- **Evening (6-8 PM PT):** Vulnerable moments/community questions

**Golden Rules:**
1. **Never self-promote in the tweet body** - Product link only in bio
2. **Never use hashtags** - They scream "trying too hard"
3. **Never post screenshots of analytics** - Focus on building, not metrics
4. **Always respond to comments** - Within 2 hours if possible

## 7. The Long Game: When Can You "Sell"?

**Phase 1 (Months 1-3): Pure Value**
- 0% product mentions in tweets
- 100% building in public + teaching
- Goal: 500+ followers of fellow developers

**Phase 2 (Months 4-6): Soft Context**
- Occasional "I built X feature in LearnifyTube, here's the technical breakdown" tweets
- Product is the EXAMPLE, not the PITCH
- Goal: 1,500+ followers, regular engagement

**Phase 3 (Months 7+): Natural Promotion**
- By now, people KNOW what you're building
- They ask about it
- You can share "LearnifyTube v2.0 is out" as a milestone, not a pitch
- Goal: Community-driven growth

**Key Metric:** When people start tweeting about your project WITHOUT you asking, you've won.

## 10. Measurement (But Don't Obsess)

**Track Weekly:**
- Follower count (growth rate, not absolute number)
- Engagement rate (replies/likes per tweet)
- Profile clicks (how many people check your bio?)
- GitHub stars/traffic from X

**Ignore:**
- Viral tweets (one-hit wonders don't build trust)
- Comparison to other accounts (your journey is unique)
- Follower count of non-engaged accounts (bots/inactive users)

**Success Indicator:**
You know it's working when:
1. People reply with technical discussions (not just "nice!")
2. Other devs @ you when someone asks about Electron/React
3. Your GitHub issues come from X followers
4. You get DMs asking for advice (not pitching you)
